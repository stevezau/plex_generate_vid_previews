What is tone mapping?
HDR (High Dynamic Range) videos have a wider range of brightness than a standard display can show. Tone mapping converts those brightness levels so thumbnails look correct on any screen. This setting only affects HDR content (HDR10, HLG, Dolby Vision).
Which should I pick?
  • Hable — The Plex default. Gives a filmic, cinematic look that preserves detail in both shadows and highlights. Can look slightly darker than the original.
  • Reinhard — Produces the brightest thumbnails, closest to how standard (non-HDR) content looks. Try this first if your thumbnails look too dark.
  • Mobius — A good middle ground: keeps colors natural and accurate with good brightness. Slightly less bright than Reinhard.
  • Clip — Keeps colours perfectly accurate for normal brightness, but cuts off very bright highlights sharply.
  • Gamma — Makes everything noticeably brighter, but can wash out colours and reduce contrast.
  • Linear — A simple brightness stretch. Can look flat or washed out on some content.
